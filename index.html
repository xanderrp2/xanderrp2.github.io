About me

Hello! My name is Xander, I (the one in the pink suit) am currently a first year at the University of Waterloo pursuing a 5 year Co-op degree in Computing and Financial Management. This is essentially a Computer Science and Finance double major under a single degree. I am currently writing to express my interest in a possible Co-op/internship position within your organization. With a solid foundation in both Computer Science and Finance, a decade of hands-on experience in programming and technology, and a passion for financial innovation, I am eager to contribute my skills to a dynamic team.

Throughout my academic and professional journey, I have consistently committed to excellence in both computer science and finance. My 10+ years of programming education in languages such as Python, Java, C, and SQL, started long before my university career and continues to burn with just as much passion. coupled with experience in machine learning and data science, I believe this positions me as a versatile candidate capable of tackling complex challenges.

In addition to my technical acumen, my background in finance, as well as my involvement in projects such as a mental health app and robot portfolio-maker, have equipped me with a well-rounded understanding of the intersection between technology, finance, and accounting. I am excited about the prospect of leveraging my skills to drive innovation and efficiency within your organization.

While I know there are many highly qualified candidates out there, I feel as though you need to know what I bring to the table and what can make me a valuable asset to your team. Firstly, I am a massive team player. As a varsity athlete who has competed within various teams for many years, I know how to be not only a member of a team but a part of that team's success. I know that a team is only as strong as its members, so whether it be asking for help when needed, or helping others, I am never afraid to take the initiative.

Secondly, working towards a degree is something I would consider to be fun and challenging. However, I know that theory is just a starting point. From there I can dive into my true passion for learning and developing new skills. What I mean by this is that I’ll never be the first one to give up on a problem, especially if there's something new to be learned. I’ll always do my best to determine the most effective and efficient solution to a problem. And if I can’t figure it out? I’ll do some research, try to learn, or ask for help. That is why I am in a Co-op program after all.

I know that you’re probably very busy, so I would like to thank you very much for taking the time to read this letter. I look forward to hearing from you and wish you all the best.

Thank you and have a great day!
